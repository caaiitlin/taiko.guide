# Compact Controllers

Compact controllers provide an affordable and convenient way to play Taiko no Tatsujin at home. Their smaller size, lower cost, and portability make them the most popular choice for home players, while modern designs are capable of supporting everything from casual play to high-level performance.

This page explains what makes compact controllers different, who they're best suited for, and provides an overview of the most popular models currently available.

# What Are Compact Controllers?

Compact controllers are smaller drum controllers designed primarily for home use. Compared to arcade-style controllers (ASCs), they occupy less space, are easier to transport, and are significantly more affordable, making them the controller type most players begin with.

Despite their smaller size, most compact controllers are capable of supporting very high-level play. Many experienced players continue to use them thanks to their portability and excellent value.

Compared to ASCs, compact controllers are typically:

- Smaller and lighter
- Designed for tabletop play
- Easier to transport and store
- More affordable
- Available in a wider range of price points

These advantages come with some compromises. Their smaller playing surfaces require more precise positioning than a full-sized arcade drum, and most use plastic rather than the wooden construction found in ASCs. As a result, they generally provide a less authentic arcade experience, although newer premium compact controllers have narrowed this gap considerably.

## Should You Buy One?

For many players, a compact controller is the best place to start.

Compact controllers are considerably more affordable than ASCs, require very little space, and can easily be packed away when not in use. They are ideal for players with limited space or anyone looking for a practical home setup.

If your primary goal is to recreate the arcade experience as closely as possible, an ASC may be the better choice. However, for the vast majority of players, a compact controller offers the best balance of performance, price, and convenience.

## Featured Controllers

The controllers below are among the most established compact controllers currently available. Each has been widely used and independently reviewed by the Taiko community, making them the easiest to research and compare.

Numerous other compact controllers are also available, including the IINE Tatacon, DOBE Tatacon, and various generic alternatives. While these are often cheaper, they generally offer fewer features, lower build quality, or a poorer playing experience. They are briefly discussed later in this guide.

# HORI Tatacon

The **HORI Tatacon** is the official Taiko no Tatsujin controller produced under licence from Bandai Namco. Available for multiple console generations, it has introduced countless players to home Taiko and remains one of the most popular entry-level controllers available.

| ![HORI Tatacon models](/assets/images/controllers/hori-tatacons.png) | 
|:--:| 
| *The current lineup: Nintendo Switch, PlayStation 5, and Xbox Series X\|S versions of the HORI Tatacon.* |

## Pros

- Officially licensed by Bandai Namco
- Widely available and generally easy to purchase
- Affordable compared to most dedicated drum controllers
- Large community with extensive setup, troubleshooting, and modification resources
- Compatible with official console releases

## Cons

- Drum surface provides noticeably less rebound than newer compact controllers
- Lower stock sensitivity than newer compact controllers
- Considerably louder than other compact controllers
- Often benefits from community modifications

## Good to Know

- Features a **23 cm (9 in.)** drum surface
- Available in Nintendo Switch, PlayStation 5, and Xbox Series X\|S versions
- PS5 and Xbox versions feature native PC support via a mode switch on the rear of the controller
- One of the most heavily modified Taiko controllers, with extensive community guides available
- Replacement parts and aftermarket modifications are widely available
- A well-modded HORI Tatacon can provide a dramatically better playing experience than stock
- Ships with a pair of plastic bachi

## Best For

Beginners looking for an affordable first controller, particularly console players, who don't mind modifying the controller later to improve its performance.

# Rythmagica TDC10

The **Rythmagica TDC10** is a premium compact controller designed to deliver the best possible playing experience without stepping up to a full-sized ASC. It combines excellent responsiveness, active firmware development, and modern software support in a compact design.

| ![Rythmagica TDC10](/assets/images/controllers/tdc10.png) | 
|:--:| 
| *The TDC10, with its included “C3” bachi.* |

## Pros

- Uses piezo sensors, contributing to excellent stock sensitivity compared to HORI Tatacons
- Quieter than the HORI Tatacon
- Regular firmware updates with ongoing feature improvements
- **TDC Mate** companion app allows for firmware updates and controller configuration
- Excellent build quality with a polished plug-and-play experience

## Cons

- Can be more expensive than entry-level compact controllers (region-dependent)
- Less widely available than the HORI Tatacon

## Good to Know

- Features a **25 cm (~10 in.)** drum surface
- Supports multiple platforms
- Manufactured by Rythmagica, the company behind the TDC17
- Continues to receive active firmware updates
- TDC Mate app connects via Bluetooth and runs on Android and iOS devices
- Ships with a pair of Rythmagica "C3" bachi

## Best For

Players looking for a high-performing compact controller who value excellent responsiveness, active software support, and a seamless out-of-the-box experience.

# IINE Pro Max

The **IINE Pro Max** is a large compact controller designed for players who want a more arcade-like playing experience without stepping up to a full-sized ASC. Its **35 cm (~14 in.)** drum surface offers significantly more room than traditional compact controllers while remaining considerably smaller, lighter, and more affordable than an ASC.

| ![IINE Pro Max](/assets/images/controllers/iinepm.png) | 
|:--:| 
| *The IINE Pro Max alongside a pair of included bachi.* |

## Pros

- Significantly larger drum surface than most compact controllers
- Uses piezo sensors, contributing to excellent stock sensitivity compared to HORI Tatacons
- Quieter than the HORI Tatacon
- Excellent value for money
- Larger playing surface makes positioning more forgiving

## Cons

- Some owners have reported the rubber drum surface lifting up over time
- Smaller owner community than the HORI Tatacon
- Fewer long-term reviews than more established controllers

## Good to Know

- The largest compact controller currently available, measuring **35 cm (~14 in.)**
- Supports multiple platforms
- Offers a playing experience that sits between a traditional Tatacon and a full-sized ASC
- Ships with a pair of generic wooden bachi

## Best For

Players looking for a larger controller that offers a more spacious playing experience without the cost or space requirements of an ASC.

# Other Compact Controllers

For most players, these controllers are not recommended. Spending slightly more on a HORI Tatacon or one of the featured controllers above will generally provide a much better long-term experience.

## IINE Tatacon (Risecon)

The **IINE Tatacon**, also known as the **IINE Risecon**, is a competent budget alternative to the HORI Tatacon, but offers few compelling advantages. 

| ![IINE Tatacon](/assets/images/controllers/iine-tatacon.png) | 
|:--:| 
| *The IINE Tatacon bears a striking resemblance to the HORI Tatacon, though slightly larger.* |

Performance is generally comparable to a stock HORI Tatacon, although it has a much smaller owner community and fewer long-term reviews. Unless the IINE Tatacon can be found at a significantly lower price, the HORI Tatacon is generally the safer purchase thanks to its larger community, greater availability of modification guides and parts, and more established reputation.

## DOBE and Other Off-Brand Controllers

Numerous inexpensive drum controllers are sold under brands such as **DOBE**, **Armor3**, and various generic manufacturers.

| ![Generic tatacons](/assets/images/controllers/generic-tatacons.png) | 
|:--:| 
| *Most generic controllers, such as the DOBE and Armor3 drums (above), lack any unique design elements.* |

While these controllers may appear similar to the featured models on this page, their internal construction differs significantly. Most consist of a hollow plastic drum with only a handful of small rubber dome switches beneath the playing surface. Unlike higher-quality controllers, the majority of the drum surface contains no sensors.

This results in poor sensitivity, inconsistent hit detection, and noticeable dead zones, making it difficult to play accurately or consistently. Although these controllers are often significantly cheaper than other alternatives, the poorer playing experience is usually not worth the savings.
