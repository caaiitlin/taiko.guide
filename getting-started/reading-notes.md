# Reading Notes

Every song in Taiko no Tatsujin is built from a small number of note types. 
Once you understand what each note represents and how to play it, you'll have everything you need to start playing songs.

There are only **two basic inputs** in Taiko no Tatsujin:

- **Don (D)**: Strike the **surface (drumhead)** of the drum.
- **Ka (K)**: Strike the **rim** of the drum.

Every other note in the game is simply a variation of one of these two inputs.

> **Note:** Unless otherwise stated, this guide describes the behaviour of the **modern Nijiiro arcade version**. Some mechanics differ slightly on home versions.

# Basic Notes

## 🔴 Don Note

*[Insert image]*

The **Don note** is represented by a **red circle**. 

To play it, strike the **surface (drumhead)** of the drum when the note reaches the judgement circle. 

Either side of the drumhead may be used. 

**Remember**: red notes always represent **Don**. 

## 🔵 Ka Note

*[Insert image]*

The **Ka note** is represented by a **blue circle**. 

To play it, strike the **rim** of the drum when the note reaches the judgement circle. 

Either side of the rim may be used. 

**Remember**: blue notes always represent **Ka**. 

## 🔴🔵 Large Don & Ka Notes

*[Insert image]*

**Large Notes** are represented by **larger versions of the standard Don and Ka notes**. 

They are played using the same areas of the drum as their regular counterparts: 

- 🔴 **Large Don**: Strike the **surface (drumhead)**. 
- 🔵 **Large Ka**: Strike the **rim**.

On **Nijiiro** arcade cabinets, the larger note indicates a **strong strike**.
However, most arcade cabinets are configured with sensitivity settings that allow Large Notes to register with a normal-strength hit,
so many players simply play them the same way as regular Don and Ka notes.  

> **PC/Console versions:** Large Notes are intended to be played by pressing **both corresponding inputs simultaneously** rather than using a strong strike.
>
> - 🔴 **Large Don**: Press both Don inputs together.
> - 🔵 **Large Ka**: Press both Ka inputs together.
>
> Using both inputs awards the highest possible score on these versions.

## 🟡 Drum Rolls

*[Insert image]*
  
**Drum Rolls** begin with a yellow note followed by a yellow bar. There are two variants: 
- 🟡 Drum Roll
- 🟡 Large Drum Roll

Both are played in exactly the same way. When the Drum Roll reaches the judgement circle, repeatedly strike the drum until the yellow bar ends.

Unlike regular notes, **both Don and Ka hits count**, so you may strike either the surface or the rim.

Like Large Don and Large Ka notes, a **Large Drum Roll** is intended to be played with **strong strikes**.

In practice, most arcade cabinets are configured with sensitivity settings that allow normal-strength hits to register successfully.
Because of this, most players simply play Large Drum Rolls the same way as regular Drum Rolls.

**Remember**: 
- Drum Rolls are **not judged for timing**. 
- Both Don and Ka hits count. 
- Generally: faster hits = more hits = more points.

> **Scoring:** Large Drum Rolls award more points per hit compared to regular Drum Rolls, but are otherwise played identically.
