# Bachi

**Maibachi** (マイバチ, literally "my sticks"), also known simply as **bachi,** are personal drumsticks designed for playing Taiko no Tatsujin. Unlike the bachi provided at most arcades, called “house bachi,” maibachi are lighter, more consistent, and come in a wide range of woods, sizes, and designs.

Using your own bachi isn't required to enjoy the game, and many players spend hundreds of hours using house bachi before buying their own. As your skills improve, however, a well-made pair can offer greater comfort, consistency, and control.

There is no single "best" pair of bachi. Different players prefer different woods, lengths, weights, and grip styles depending on their technique and the types of charts they enjoy playing.

This guide explains the factors that affect how bachi feel and how to choose a pair that suits you.

**Remember:** Buying expensive bachi won't instantly improve your scores. Skill comes from practice - a comfortable pair simply allows you to play more consistently over time.
